11/**
12 * @namespace svOneD
13 * @brief 3D-1D coupling subroutines.
14 *
15 * These routines interface the 3D finite-element solver svMultiPhysics with
16 * the 1D blood-flow solver svOneDSolver via a dynamically loaded shared
17 * library, such as libsvOneDSolver_interface.so or libsvOneDSolver_interface.dylib.
18 *
19 * @par Coupling overview
20 * NEU coupling, where the 1D inlet is driven by the 3D outflow:
21 * - 3D to 1D: flow rate Q, passed through params[3] and params[4].
22 * - 1D to 3D: pressure P, returned through cpl_value.
23 * - 3D boundary condition: Neumann pressure traction.
24 *
25 * DIR coupling, where the 1D outlet is driven by the 3D pressure:
26 * - 3D to 1D: pressure P, passed through params[3] and params[4].
27 * - 1D to 3D: flow rate Q, returned through cpl_value.
28 * - 3D boundary condition: Dirichlet velocity profile.
29 *
30 * @par Parallelism model
31 * Unlike the 0D solver, which is solved once on the master rank, each 1D model
32 * is independent and has its own input file. Multiple 1D models are therefore
33 * read, initialized, and solved in parallel.
34 *
35 * Initialization in init_svOneD() has two phases:
36 * - Parallel initialization:
37 * - Collect all svOneD-coupled faces into a list indexed from 0 to N-1.
38 * - Assign face/model k to MPI rank k % nProcs.
39 * - Each rank reads and initializes only its owned model(s), with no MPI
40 * synchronization, so all ranks work simultaneously.
41 * - Batch metadata exchange:
42 * - After all ranks finish initializing their owned model(s), share
43 * system_size and coupled_dof via MPI_Bcast so that all ranks know the
44 * sizes needed for subsequent result broadcasts.
45 *
46 * Time stepping in calc_svOneD() also has two phases:
47 * - Parallel solve:
48 * - Each rank runs the 1D solve for its owned model(s), with no MPI calls,
49 * so different models can run concurrently on different ranks.
50 * - Batch result exchange:
51 * - After all ranks finish solving, results are shared via MPI_Bcast so that
52 * all ranks know each result and can update the corresponding coupled
53 * boundary condition value.
54 *
55 * @par Parameters passed to run_1d_simulation_step_1d_
56 * - params[0]: number of time points, currently 2.0.
57 * - params[1]: t_old, the time at the start of the step.
58 * - params[2]: t_new, the time at the end of the step.
59 * - params[3]: BC_val_old, the coupled Q or P value at t_old.
60 * - params[4]: BC_val_new, the coupled Q or P value at t_new.
61 */
